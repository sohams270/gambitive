The rollout everyone in fintech has been waiting eighteen months for is now live at three major banks, with two more expected before the end of the quarter.

What happened: banks can now extend pre-approved credit lines directly over UPI, which means a lending product with zero card infrastructure and near-zero distribution cost.

Why it matters: every fintech pitch deck from the last two years assumed they would own this customer relationship. The banks just demonstrated they can ship it themselves.

The second-order effect to watch is collections. Credit on a payment rail with no statement cycle behaves differently from a credit card, and nobody has public data on repayment behaviour yet.

Our read: the fintechs that survive this are the ones that stop pretending to be lenders and start selling underwriting and collections capability to the banks that now own the rail.
