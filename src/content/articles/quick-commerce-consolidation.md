Two regional quick-commerce players wound down tier-2 operations this week, closing roughly 60 dark stores between them.

What happened: the expansion thesis was that tier-2 density would follow tier-1 patterns with a lag. It didn't. Basket sizes stayed 35% lower while last-mile costs stayed the same.

Why it matters: national players are watching the same dashboards. Expect quiet retreats dressed up as "network optimisation" over the next two quarters.

Our read: 10-minute delivery is a real business in maybe eight Indian cities. Everywhere else, it's a subsidy with a countdown timer.
