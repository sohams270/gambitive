Third quarter in a row: global capability centres added more senior engineering headcount than venture-funded startups did, and the gap is widening.

What happened: GCC hiring grew 23% year on year while startup hiring stayed flat. The premium for a staff engineer at a GCC now sits around 40% over the median startup offer, with none of the equity risk.

Why it matters: the standard startup pitch to senior talent — take a pay cut, get equity upside — was built for a market where the alternative was a services company. It wasn't built for this.

Our read: startups won't win this on cash. The ones still hiring well are selling scope and speed, and being honest about the equity math instead of hand-waving it.
